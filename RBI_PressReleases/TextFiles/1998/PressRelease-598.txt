April 01, 1998 
Foreign Currency Assets (FCA) increase by US$ 3.5 billion in 1997-98 
During the year 1997-98, the foreign currency assets of the Reserve Bank of India 
increased by US$ 3.5 billion. On March 31, 1998, foreign currency assets were US$ 
25.9 billion as compared with US$ 22.4 billion on March 31, 1997.  
Inclusive of gold and SDR, the total foreign exchange reserves stood at US# 29.3 billion 
on March 31, 1998 representing in increase of US$ 2.9 billion over March 31, 1997. This 
takes into account revaluation of gold reserves due to fall in the international price of 
gold.  
Since January 16, 1998, when the Reserve Bank of India had announced a number of 
monetary measures to stabilise the forex market, the foreign currency assets have 
increased by US$ 2.5 billion. The RBI reference rate was US$ 1 = Rs. 39.50 on March 
31, 1998 as compared with Rs. 40.36 as on January 16, 1998. The six month forward 
premium on March 31, 1998 was 7.2 per cent after having touched a high of 20.2 per 
cent on January 21, 1998.  
Net of outstanding forward sales, the foreign currency assets were US$ 24.2 billion on 
March 31, 1998 against a corresponding figure of US$ 22.0 billion on March 31, 1997, 
showing an increase of US$ 2.2 billion over the year. Compared with the position on 
January 16, 1998 the increase in foreign currency assets (net of outstanding forward 
sales) was US$ 3.1 billion.
