Auctions of Government of India 7-year Stock
and 10-year Stock announced
April 2, 2002
Government of India have announced sale by auction of a new 7-year
Government Stock for Rs.3,000 crore (nominal) and a new 10-year Government Stock
for Rs.4,000 crore (nominal).
Both the auctions will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai
Office, Fort, Mumbai on Thursday, April 4, 2002 using "Uniform Price Auction" method.
Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve Bank of
India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort, Mumbai should be submitted at that
office on April 4, 2002 during banking hours. On the basis of bids received, the Reserve
Bank of India will determine the maximum rate of yield at which offers will be accepted.
The results of the auctions will be announced on April 4, 2002 and payment by successful
bidders will be during banking hours on April 5, 2002. The new stocks will qualify for
the Ready Forward facility.
Under "Uniform Price Auction" method, competitive bids offered with rates up to
and including the maximum rate of yield, as determined by the Reserve Bank of India,
will be accepted at the maximum rate of yield so determined. Bids quoting higher than
the maximum rate of yield determined by the Reserve Bank of India will be rejected.
For both the auctions, Government Stock up to 5 per cent of the notified amount
of the sale will be allotted to eligible individuals and Institutions under the Scheme for
Non-Competitive Bidding Facility in the Auction of Government Securities.
Bids for underwriting the issues of Government Securities under auctions can be
submitted by the 'Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market' up to 2.30 p.m
on April 3, 2002 to the CGM-In-Charge, Internal Debt Management Cell, Reserve Bank
of India, Central Office, Fort, Mumbai 400 001.
