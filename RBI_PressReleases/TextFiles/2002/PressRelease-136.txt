GoI announces the sale (re-issue) of “8.35% 2022” through price based auction
December 2, 2002
The Government of India has announced the sale (re-issue) of “8.35 per cent
Government Stock 2022” for a notified amount of Rs.5,000 crore through a price
based auction using multiple price auction method.
Up to 5% of the notified amount of the sale of the Stock will be allotted to
eligible individuals and Institutions as per the Scheme for Non-Competitive Bidding
Facility in the Auction of Government Securities.
The auction will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) at Mumbai
on Thursday, December 5, 2002.  Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the
Regional Director, Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort,
Mumbai-400 001 should be submitted to that Office on Thursday, December 5, 2002.
The competitive bids by the members of Negotiated Dealing System (NDS) should be
submitted only in electronic format using NDS, while all the non-competitive bids
should be submitted in the prescribed physical form. All bids should be submitted by
12.30 P.M. On the basis of bids received, RBI will determine the minimum price at
which offers will be accepted.
The results of the auction will be announced on December 5, 2002 and
payment by successful bidders will be during banking hours on December 6, 2002
(Friday). The stock will qualify for the ready forward facility.
Bids for underwriting of the Government Security under auction can be
submitted by the Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market up to 12.30
p.m. on December 4, 2002 (Wednesday) to the Chief General Manager, Internal Debt
Management Cell, Reserve Bank of India, Central Office, Fort, Mumbai 400 001.
It may be recalled that as per the calendar for issuance of Government
securities the notified amount for the auction scheduled for December 2-7, 2002 was
Rs. 4,000 crore for a security with tenure of 15-20 years. However, taking into
account all relevant factors, it has been decided to issue re-issue a 20- year security
for increased amount of Rs. 5,000 crore instead of Rs. 4,000 crore.
