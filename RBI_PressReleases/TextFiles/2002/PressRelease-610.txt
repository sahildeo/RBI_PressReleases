Government of India announces the sale of two dated securities
through yield based auctions
August 22, 2002
The Government of India have announced the sale of a new 15 year Stock for a
notified amount of Rs.5,000 crore (nominal) through a yield based auction using multiple
price method. Government of India have also announced the sale of a new 30 year Stock
for a notified amount of Rs.2,000 crore (nominal) through a yield based auction using
uniform price method. The auctions will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India,
Mumbai Office, Fort, Mumbai on August 27, 2002 (Tuesday).
 Up to 5% of the notified amount of the sale of both the Stocks will be allotted to
eligible individuals and Institutions as per the Scheme for Non-Competitive Bidding
Facility in the Auction of Government Securities.
Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve Bank
of India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort, Mumbai-400 001 should be
submitted to that Office on August 27, 2002 during banking hours. The competitive bids
by the NDS members should be submitted only in electronic format using NDS, while all
the non-competitive bids should be submitted in the prescribed physical form.
The results of the auction will be announced on August 27, 2002 and payment by
successful bidders will be during banking hours on August 28, 2002 (Wednesday).  Both
the stock will qualify for the ready forward facility.
Bids for underwriting of the Government Securities under auction can be
submitted by the ‘Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market’ up to 2.30 p.m.
on August 26 2002 (Monday) to the Chief General Manager, Internal Debt Management
Cell, Reserve Bank of India, Central Office, Fort, Mumbai 400 001.
It may be recalled that as per the indicative calendar for issuance of Government
securities the notified amounts and the tenure of the securities for the auction scheduled
for August 26-30, 2002 were Rs.6,000 crore for a security with tenure of 15 years and
Rs.2,000 crore for a security with tenure of 20 years.  However, taking into account all
relevant factors, it has been decided to issue a new 15-year Stock for reduced amount of
Rs.5,000 crore instead of Rs. 6,000 crore and a new 30-year Stock for Rs.2,000 crore
instead of a 20-year Stock for Rs.2,000 crore.
