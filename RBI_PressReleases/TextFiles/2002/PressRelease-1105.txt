Auction of Government of India Stocks announced
May 9, 2002
The Government of India have announced the sale by auction of two new
Government Stocks of 8 year and 20 year maturity for Rs.3,000 crore (nominal)
each.
Both the auctions will be yield based and will be conducted by the
Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office, Fort, Mumbai on Monday, May 13, 2002
using "Multiple Price Auction" method.  Bids in the prescribed form obtainable
from the Regional Director, Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt
Office), Fort, Mumbai should be submitted at that Office on May 13, 2002 during
banking hours.
The competitive bids by the Negotiated Dealing System (NDS) members
should be submitted only in electronic format using NDS, while all the non-
competitive bids should be submitted in the prescribed physical form.  On the
basis of bids received, Reserve Bank of India will determine the maximum rate of
yield at which offers will be accepted.  The results of the auctions will be
announced on May 13, 2002 and payment by successful bidders will be during
banking hours on May 14, 2002.  The new Stocks will qualify for the Ready
Forward facility.
Both the Government Stocks up to 5 per cent of the notified amount of the
sale will be allotted to eligible individuals and Institutions under the Scheme for
Non-Competitive Bidding Facility in the Auction of Government Securities.
Bids for underwriting the issue of Government Securities under auction
can be submitted by the 'Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market'
up to 12.30 p.m. on Saturday.  May 11, 2002 to the CGM-in-Charge, Internal
Debt Management Cell, Reserve Bank of India, Central Office, Mumbai - 400
001.
