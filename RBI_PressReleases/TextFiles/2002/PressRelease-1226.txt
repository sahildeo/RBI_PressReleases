Auction of Government of India 15-year Stock announced
April 11, 2002
The Government of India have announced sale by auction of a new 15-year
Government Stock for Rs 6,000 crore (nominal).
The auction will be yield based and will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of
India, Mumbai Office, Fort, Mumbai on Monday, April 15, 2002 using “Multiple Price
Auction” method. Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director,
Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort, Mumbai should be
submitted at that office on April 15, 2002 during banking hours. The competitive bids by
the Negotiated Dealing System (NDS) members should be submitted only in electronic
format using NDS, while all the non-competitive bids should be submitted in the
prescribed physical form. On the basis of bids received, the Reserve Bank of India will
determine the maximum rate of yield at which offers will be accepted. The results of the
auctions will be announced on April 15, 2002 and payment by successful bidders will be
during banking hours on April 16, 2002. The new stock will qualify for the Ready
Forward facility.
Government Stock up to 5 per cent of the notified amount of the sale will be
allotted to eligible individuals and Institutions under the Scheme for Non-Competitive
Bidding Facility in the Auction of Government Securities.
Bids for underwriting the issues of Government Securities under auction can be
submitted by the ‘Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market’ up to 2.30 p.m.
on April 12, 2002 to the CGM-In-Charge, Internal Debt Management Cell, Reserve Bank
of India, Central Office, Mumbai 400 001.
Alpana Killawala
