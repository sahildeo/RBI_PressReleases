Investment by FIIs under PIS  
 
The Reserve Bank of India has today notified that the percentage of foreign 
holdings by Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) under Portfolio Investment Scheme 
(PIS) in under noted banks/company have gone down below of their paid up capital 
shown against their names. The restriction placed on the purchase of shares of the 
banks/ company on behalf of FIIs may be treated as withdrawn with immediate 
effect. It will be therefore now be in order to purchase the shares of the following 
banks/company in the secondary market who have permission to invest under the 
Portfolio Investment Scheme subject to the existing ceilings and guidelines in this 
regard.  
1) Karur Vysya Bank Limited   22 per cent 
2) ING Vysya Bank     72 per cent 
3) Jindal South West Holdings Limited  22 per cent
