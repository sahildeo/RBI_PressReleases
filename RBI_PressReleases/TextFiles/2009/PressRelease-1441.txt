March 26, 2009 
Issuance Calendar for Marketable Dated Securities 
 
  With a view to enabling institutional and retail investors to plan their investment 
in a better manner and also for providing transparency and stability in the Government 
securities market, it has been decided to continue with the system of releasing an 
indicative calendar for issuance of Government of India securities. It may be noted that 
Government of India securities amounting to Rs. 33,089 crore are due for redemption in 
the first half of the year 2009-2010.  Furthermore, estimated coupon payments in the first 
half of 2009-2010 aggregate to Rs. 67,924 crore. 
  Simultaneously, a separate Press Release is being released by the Reserve 
Bank of India regarding the Open Market Operations (OMO) and unwinding of MSS 
securities.   
  The following indicative issuance calendar for issuance of the dated securities 
for the first half of the year 2009-2010 covering the period from April 1, 2009 to 
September 30, 2009, as given below, is being issued in consultation with the 
Government of India.

 As hitherto, all the auctions covered by the calendar will have the facility of non-
competitive bidding scheme under which five per cent of the notified amount will be 
reserved for the specified retail investors. 
 Variable rate securities may be issued depending upon the market conditions. 
 As in the past, the Central Government/Reserve Bank will continue to have the 
flexibility to bring about modifications in the above calendar in terms of notified amount, 
issuance period, maturity, etc., keeping in view the emerging requirements of the 
Government, market conditions and other relevant factors after giving due notice.
