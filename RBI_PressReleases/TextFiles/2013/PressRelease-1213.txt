RBI Announces OMO Sale of Government Securities  
for ` 12,000 crore on July 18, 2013 
As part of various measures announced on July 15, 2013 to address exchange 
rate volatility, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has decided to conduct sale of 
government securities under its open market operations (OMO). Accordingly, it has been 
decided to conduct OMO sale auction of the following government securities for an 
aggregate amount of ` 12,000 crore through multi-security auction using the multiple 
price method: 
Sr. No Security Maturity Date 
1. 8.07% GS 2017 July 3, 2017 
2. 8.15% GS 2022 June 11, 2022 
3. 8.33% GS 2026 July  9, 2026 
4. 8.97% GS 2030 December 5, 2030 
The auction shall be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India, Financial Markets 
Department, Central Office, Mumbai on July 18, 2013 (Thursday). 
Market participants may take note of the following: 
There is an overall aggregate ceiling amount of ` 12,000 crore for all the 
securities in the basket put together. There is no security-wise notified amount. 
RBI reserves the right to: 
? Decide on the quantum of sale of individual securities  
? Accept bids for less than the aggregate amount of `12,000 crore  
? Sell marginally higher than the aggregate amount due to rounding effects. 
? Accept or reject any or all of the bids either wholly or partially if deemed fit without   
assigning reason. 
The eligible participants should submit their bids in electronic format on the 
Reserve Bank of India Core Banking Solution (E-Kuber) system between 10.30 a.m. and 
12.00 noon on July 18, 2013 (Thursday). Any physical bid should be submitted to 
Mumbai Office (Public Accounts Department) in the prescribed form obtainable from the 
Regional Director, Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office, Fort, Mumbai-400001 and/or 
RBI website (www.rbi.org.in). The result of the auction will be announced on the same 
day. 
The successful bidders will be required to make payment to RBI on July 19, 2013 
(Friday) by forenoon.
