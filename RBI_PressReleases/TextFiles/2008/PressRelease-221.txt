RBI expands the coverage of its “Database on Indian Economy (DBIE)” 
The Reserve Bank of India has further expanded the coverage of its "Database on 
Indian Economy", an on-line database built on the data warehousing technology with 
enhanced functionality. The coverage has been increased to include more static reports on 
outward remittances under Liberalised Remittance Scheme for resident individuals, foreign 
exchange liquidity, ownership details of central and state government securities, quarterly 
estimate of GDP by expenditure approach and industry-wise details of Index of Industrial 
Production (IIP). 
The additional reports being released through the Database on Indian Economy are: 
1. Outward Remittances under the Liberalised Remittance Scheme  for Resident 
Individuals – Monthly  
2. Outward Remittances under the  Liberalised Remittance Scheme for Resident 
Individuals – Yearly  
3. Predetermined Short-term net drains on foreign currency assets (Nominal value) 
4. Contingent short- term net drains on foreign currency assets (Nominal value) 
5. Share of SDR Basket in Forex Reserves – Annual  
6. Ownership of Central Government securities 
7. Ownership of State Government securities 
8. Ownership of Central and State Government securities 
9. Quarterly Estimates of GDP, Expenditure (Constant Price)  
10. Quarterly Estimates of GDP, Expenditure (Current Price) 
11. IIP - Seventeen Major Industry groups of Manufacturing Sector – Monthly 
12. IIP - Seventeen Major Industry groups of Manufacturing Sector - Yearly 
With the release of these data series, the Database on Indian Economy would now 
have 216 static reports (subject and frequency wise), 74 subject-wise and 53 frequency-wise 
data query templates covering various sectors of the Indian economy. The URL for 
accessing the Database on Indian Economy (DBIE) is http://dbie.rbi.org.in. It will also 
continue to be available on its website (www.rbi.org.in) under the “Database” Section as 
earlier. 
 Economists, statisticians, data analysts and market participants may find this site 
more useful with the increased coverage. We welcome users’ feedback which is considered 
valuable in bringing out continuous improvements in the coverage of data series in the 
system.
