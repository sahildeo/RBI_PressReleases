RBI expands Coverage of its “Database on Indian Economy” 
 
The Reserve Bank of India has further expanded the coverage of its "Database on Indian 
Economy", an on-line database built on the data warehousing technology with enhanced 
functionality. The coverage has been enhanced to include static reports on directory of urban co-
operative banks, commercial banks deposit details and important data on external sectors with 
quarterly international investment positions.  The additional reports being released through the 
Database on Indian Economy are:  
 
1. Performance of private limited companies – Selected Industries  
2. Maturity Pattern wise Deposits of SCBs  - According to State  
3. Maturity Pattern wise Deposits of SCBs – According to Bank Group  
4. Percentage Distribution of Deposits of SCBs according to Interest rate  
5. Top two hundred centres arranged according to size of Gross bank credit of SCBs  
6. Top two hundred centres arranged according to size of aggregate deposits of SCBs  
7. International Investment Position - Quarterly  
8. Forex Rates - Month-High  / Month-Low  
9. Forward Premia (Inter-Bank) - Monthly Average  
10. Profile of Banks  
11. Weighted Average Call Money Rate  
12. Turnover In the Equity Derivative Market  
13. Directory of Urban Co-operative Banks  
 
With the release of these data series, the Database on Indian Economy would now have 
194 static reports (subject and frequency wise), 74 subject wise and 53 frequency wise data query 
templates covering various sectors of the Indian economy.     
The Reserve Bank of India's Database on Indian Economy is available on its website 
(www.rbi.org.in) under the Database Section. This can also be directly accessed through URL 
https://reservebank.org.in  The coverage of the Database is continuously being expanded on the 
basis of the feedback received from the users of the database. Feedback is received, among 
other things, through a feedback link on the site.
