December 23, 2008 
RBI's Special Fixed Rate Term Repo Auction for Rs. 55,620 Crore Today 
The Reserve Bank of India shall conduct a special fixed rate term repo at 6.5 per cent per 
annum against eligible securities for Rs. 55,620 crore on December 23, 2008, due for reversal on 
January 06, 2009, with a view to enabling banks to meet the liquidity requirements of Mutual 
Funds (MFs), Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and Housing Finance Companies 
(HFCs) either on incremental or on rollover basis. The outstanding amount under this facility as 
on December 22, 2008 stood at Rs. 4,380 crore 
It may be recalled that the Reserve Bank of India had announced earlier that it would 
conduct the special fixed rate term repo under Liquidity Adjustment Facility daily till June 30, 
2009 up to a cumulative amount of Rs.60,000 crore on outstanding basis and that additional 
liquidity support to the extent of up to 1.5 per cent of their NDTL, can be availed by banks 
exclusively for the purpose of meeting requirements of MFs, NBFCs and HFCs.
