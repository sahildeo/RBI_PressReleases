October 17, 2008 
Special Fixed Rate Repo under Liquidity Adjustment Facility 
The Reserve Bank of India shall conduct a special fixed rate term repo at 9 per 
cent per annum against eligible securities for Rs.14,030 crore on October 17, 2008, due 
for reversal on October 31, 2008, with a view to enabling banks to meet the liquidity 
requirements of mutual funds. 
It may be recalled that the Reserve Bank had announced earlier that it would 
conduct the special fixed rate term repo under liquidity adjustment facility every day 
until further notice up to a cumulative amount of Rs 20,000 crore for the same purpose. 
Banks have utilised Rs.3700 crore of this facility till October 15, 2008. In addition, in 
terms of further measures for improving domestic and foreign currency liquidity 
announced on October 15, 2008, banks may avail of additional liquidity support 
exclusively for the purpose of meeting the liquidity requirements of mutual funds to the 
extent of up to 0.5 per cent of their NDTL.
