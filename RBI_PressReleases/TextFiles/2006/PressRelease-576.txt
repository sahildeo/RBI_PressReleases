September 1, 2006
RBI expands coverage of its “Database on Indian Economy”
The Reserve Bank of India has today further expanded the coverage of its
"Database on Indian Economy" to include, among other things, static reports on
turnover details at National Stock Exchange (NSE) and Bombay Stock Exchange
(BSE), Central Government Market borrowings and Assets and Liabilities of a few
financial institutions.
The additional static reports released on the Database on Indian Economy
today are:
(a) Weekly traded volume in Corporate Debt at NSE,
(b) Monthly Turnover and Capitalisation at BSE,
(c) Daily Turnover and Capitalisation at BSE,
(d) Monthly Turnover and Capitalisation at NSE,
(e) Daily Turnover and Capitalisation at NSE,
(f) Month end YTM of SGL transactions in Central Government dated securities,
(g) Details of Central Government Market Borrowing by issue date,
(h) Details of Central Government Market Borrowing by maturity year,
(i) Liabilities and Assets of NABARD,
(j) Liabilities and Assets of UTI and
(k) Certificate of Deposits by SCBs
With the release of these data series, the Database on Indian Economy would
now have 150 static reports (subject and frequency wise), 67 subject wise and 53
frequency wise data query templates covering various sectors of the Indian
economy.
The Reserve Bank of India's Database on Indian Economy is available on its
website (www.rbi.org.in) under the Database Section. This can also be directly
accessed through URL https://cdbmsi.reservebank.org.in/.  The home page of the
Database on Indian Economy lists the data series available on it. The coverage of
the Database is continuously being expanded on the basis of the feedback received
from the users of the database. The present expansion is the eleventh such
expansion release.
Users are requested to provide their feedback through a feedback link on the
site so as to enable further improvements in the coverage of data series in the
system.
