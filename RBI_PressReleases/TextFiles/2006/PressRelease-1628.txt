GOI to auction 7.46% 2017 and 7.40% 2035 stocks on February 7, 2006
Government of India have announced the sale (re-issue) of "7.46 percent
Government Stock 2017" for a notified amount of Rs 3,000 crore (nominal) through a
price based auction using uniform price method. Government of India have also
announced the sale (re-issue) of “7.40 percent Government Stock 2035” for a
notified amount of Rs.3,000 crore through a price based auction using multiple price
method.  The auctions will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) at
Mumbai on Tuesday, February 7, 2006.
Up to 5 per cent of the notified amount of the sale of the stocks will be allotted
to eligible individuals and institutions as per the Scheme for Non-Competitive Bidding
Facility in the Auction of Government Securities for both the auctions.
  Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve
Bank of India, Mumbai Office, Public Debt Office, Fort, Mumbai-400 001 and RBI
website www.rbi.org.in should be submitted to that Office on Tuesday, February 7,
2006. The NDS members should submit competitive as well as non-competitive bids
in electronic format using Primary Market Operation (PMO) module of NDS. All bids
should be submitted by 12.30 p.m..
 The results of the auctions will be announced on February 7, 2006 (Tuesday)
and payment by successful bidders will be during banking hours on February 8, 2006
(Wednesday).
The Stocks will qualify for the ready forward facility.
 Bids for underwriting of the Government Securities under auction can be
submitted by the ‘Primary Dealers’ up to 12.30 p.m. on February 6, 2006 (Monday)
using PMO module of NDS and physical bids to the Regional Director, Reserve Bank
of India, Public Debt Office, Fort, Mumbai-400 001.
It may be recalled that as per the calendar for issuance of Government
securities the notified amounts and the tenure of the securities for the auctions
scheduled during February 1-8, 2006 were Rs.6,000 crore for a security with 10 year
to 14 years tenure and Rs.3,000 crore for a security with 20 year and above tenure.
However, taking into account all relevant factors, Government of India have decided
to issue the shorter tenure security for a reduced notified amount of Rs.3,000 crore
instead of Rs.6,000 crore as was announced in the calendar.
