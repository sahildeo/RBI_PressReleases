Market Stabilisation Scheme to be resumed
for Treasury bills from May 3, 2006
On a review of the liquidity conditions, it has been decided to resume
Treasury bills auctions under the Market Stabilisation Scheme (MSS) with effect
from May 3, 2006. The issuance of Treasury bills under MSS will be undertaken
by increasing the notified amount for regular weekly auctions of 91-day Treasury
Bills by Rs.1,500 crore and those for the fortnightly 182-day and 364-day
Treasury bills auctions by Rs.1,000 crore each. These auctions will continue till
further notice.
The auctions would be conducted in accordance with the annual ceiling for
2006-07 fixed at Rs.70,000 crore by the Government of India and the Reserve
Bank.
The Reserve Bank would retain the flexibility of reviewing the auctions
under the MSS from time to time, in response to evolving circumstances.
