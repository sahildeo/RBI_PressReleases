“Quarterly Statistics on Deposits and Credit of
Scheduled Commercial Banks”: September 2005
“Quarterly Statistics on Deposits and Credit of Scheduled Commercial Banks -
September 2005” provides data on aggregate deposits and gross bank credit of scheduled
commercial banks as on last Friday of September 2005. The data are based on Basic
Statistical Return-7, received from the branches of all scheduled commercial banks
(including Regional Rural Banks). During this quarter, there was amalgamation of 28
RRBs into 9 RRBs leading to reduction in number of RRBs from 196 to 177.
           The number of banked centres served by scheduled commercial banks according
to number of reporting offices stood at 34,747. Of these centres, 29,303 were single office
centres and 40 centers had 100 or more bank offices.
           The top hundred centers, out of 34,747 banked centers, arranged according to the
size of deposits accounted for 66.2 per cent of the total deposits and the top hundred
centres arranged according to the size of bank credit accounted for 75.9 per cent of total
bank credit. Aggregate deposits of top hundred centres grew at 24.2 per cent in
September 2005 over September 2004 compared to 19.6 per cent growth recorded a year
ago. Annual growth rate of gross bank credit of top hundred centres was appreciably
higher at 37.8 per cent (including the impact of IDBI Ltd.) in September 2005 over
September 2004, compared to 24.9 per cent growth recorded in September 2004.
        Nationalised Banks, as a group, accounted for 49.6 per cent of the aggregate
deposits, while State Bank of India and its Associates accounted for 24.6 per cent. The
shares of Foreign Banks, Regional Rural Banks and Other Scheduled Commercial Banks
in aggregate deposits were 4.9 per cent, 3.3 per cent and 17.7 per cent, respectively. As
regards gross bank credit, Nationalised Banks held the maximum share of 47.8 per cent in
the total bank credit followed by State Bank of India and its Associates at 23.8 per cent
and Other Scheduled Commercial Banks at 18.9 per cent. Foreign Banks and Regional
Rural Banks had relatively lower shares in the total bank credit at 6.7 per cent and 2.8 per
cent, respectively.
        At the all–India level, the credit-deposit (C-D) ratio of all scheduled commercial banks
as on the last Friday of September 2005 stood at 68.1 per cent. Among the States/Union
Territories, the highest C-D ratio was observed in Tamil Nadu (103.2 per cent), followed by
Maharashtra (96.3 per cent) and Chandigarh (94.9 per cent). At the bank group level, the
C-D ratio was above the all-India ratio in respect of Foreign Banks(93.1 per cent) and Other Scheduled Commercial Banks (72.9 per cent), and was lower for
State Bank of India and its Associates (65.9 per cent), Nationalised Banks (65.7 per cent)
and Regional Rural Banks (57.0 per cent). As regards population group-wise C-D ratio of
all scheduled commercial banks, metropolitan centres had the highest C-D ratio at 85.0
per cent, followed by rural centres (54.4 per cent) and urban centres (52.3 per cent). The
semi-urban centres recorded the lowest C-D ratio at 46.5 per cent.
         It is observed from the distribution of scheduled commercial bank offices by size of
deposits, that the offices with deposits of Rs.10 crore or more, accounted for 48.8 per cent
of the bank offices with a share of 91.8 per cent in aggregate deposits and 89.1 per cent in
total bank credit. The offices, which extended the credit of Rs.10 crore or more, accounted
for 23.3 per cent in terms of their number. These offices together accounted for 85.0 per
cent of total bank credit whereas their share in aggregate deposits was 64.0 per cent.
