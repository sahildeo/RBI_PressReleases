Silver Jubilee Inaugural Function of the Reserve Bank of India Archives 
The Reserve Bank of India Archives (RBIA), Pune celebrated its Silver Jubilee Inaugural 
function today and assumed the new name (formerly called Central Records and 
Documentation Centre). Dr. Rakesh Mohan, Deputy Governor, Reserve Bank of India unveiled 
the plaque of the RBIA and presided over the function. Located in the campus of the Reserve 
Bank of India's College of Agricultural Banking, Pune the RBIA is a centre for preservation and 
retrieval of non-current permanent records of the Reserve Bank.  
The core activities of RBIA are acquisition and scientific preservation of important 
records of the Reserve Bank in the form of files, registers etc., dating back to April 1, 1935 and 
even earlier. Photographs covering important events of the Bank, digital records, published 
material, publications brought out, newspaper clippings and securities covering period as early 
as 1777 are also preserved. The RBIA has facilities for microfilming and scanning along with 
scientific repair and rehabilitation techniques. The RBI Archives has been catering to the needs 
of historical, legal and administrative needs of the Bank as well as other institutions and 
individuals who require information.
