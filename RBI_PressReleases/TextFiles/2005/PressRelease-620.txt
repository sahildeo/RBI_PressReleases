August 11, 2005
RBI Board meets today in Mumbai
The Central Board of Directors of the Reserve Bank of India met today in
Mumbai.  The following members were present : Shri Y.H. Malegam, Prof. Mihir
Rakshit, Shri K. Madhava Rao, Shri Ratan N. Tata, Dr. Amrita Patel, Shri K.P.
Singh, Prof. V.S. Vyas, Shri D.S. Brar, Shri H.P. Ranina, Shri N.R. Narayana
Murthy, Shri Suresh Krishna, Dr. Ashok Ganguly and Government Nominee -
Shri Ashok K. Jha, Secretary, Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of
Finance. Dr. Y.V. Reddy, Governor, Reserve Bank of India chaired the meeting,
Deputy Governors of the Reserve Bank Dr. Rakesh Mohan, Smt. K.J. Udeshi,
Shri V. Leeladhar and Smt. Shyamala Gopinath were also present.  The meeting
reviewed key economic, monetary and financial developments.
The Central Board of Directors of the Reserve Bank meets seven times a
year.  Apart from holding four meetings in the four metropolises – with one
meeting in New Delhi held after the Union Budget and addressed by the Finance
Minister – the rest of the meetings are held in other state capitals by rotation.
The main function of the Central Board of Directors of the Reserve Bank is to
provide overall direction to its affairs.
