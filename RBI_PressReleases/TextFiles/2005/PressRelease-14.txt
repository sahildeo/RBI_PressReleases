December 29, 2005
Redemption of India Millennium Deposits (IMDs)
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) today completed the sale of foreign
exchange to State Bank of India (SBI) towards redemption of IMDs. A total amount
equivalent to US$ 7,079.62 million was sold out of RBI's foreign exchange reserves.
This will be reflected in the level of foreign exchange reserves as on December 30,
2005 to be published in the Weekly Statistical Supplement to the RBI Bulletin dated
January 6, 2006.
The RBI had furnished the details of sale of foreign exchange to SBI in
connection with the redemption in a Press Release dated December 28, 2005. As
indicated therein, a further sale of US$ 1,972.18 million was made on December 29,
2005.
The RBI had mentioned in an earlier Press Release dated December 13,
2005 that SBI and Government of India had made contributions towards possible
exchange losses, which are held in a Maintenance of Value Account with RBI. These
contributions have not been utilised and will be refunded.
