February 15, 2005
Tap sale of 7.17% State Government Loan, 2017
for Rs.6300 crore on February 22, 2005
Sixteen State Governments, namely, Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Bihar, Himachal
Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Kerala, Maharahstra, Manipur, Meghalaya, Nagaland,
Orissa, Punjab, Sikkim, Tripura, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal have offered to sell
7.17 per cent State Development Loan, 2017. Having maturity of twelve years, the
loans aggregating about Rs. 6300 crore will be sold on February 22,  2005 by way of
tap sale. The sale will be under the normal/debt swap market-borrowing programme
of the states. The sale will be held at Mumbai for all the 16 states as also at the
respective Regional Offices of the Reserve Bank of India.
The Reserve Bank of India reserves the right to close the sale during or after
close of banking hours on or after February 22, 2005. Successful applicants will have
to make the payment on February 24, 2005.
Applications in the prescribed form obtainable from the Public Debt Offices of
the Reserve Bank of India will be received at its Mumbai and other Regional Offices
during banking hours while the sale is open.
