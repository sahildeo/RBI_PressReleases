Government of India announces the sale of two dated securities
through auction.
October 11, 2001
The Government of India have announced sale (re-issue) by auction of “10.18
percent Government Stock, 2026” for an aggregate amount of Rs 2,000 crore (nominal).
The auction will be price based and will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India
(RBI) at Mumbai on Monday, October 15, 2001. Bids in the prescribed form obtainable
from the Regional Director, RBI, Public Debt Office, Fort, Mumbai should be submitted
to that Office on October 15, 2001, during the banking hours. On the basis of bids
received, RBI will determine the minimum price at which offers will be accepted.
The Government of India have also announced sale by auction of a new 14-year
Government Stock for Rs 6,000 crore (nominal).  The auction will be yield based and
will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) at Mumbai on Monday, October
15, 2001. Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve
Bank of India, Public Debt Office, Fort, Mumbai should be submitted to that Office on
October 15, 2001, during banking hours. On the basis of bids received, Reserve Bank of
India will determine the maximum rate of yield at which offers will be accepted.
  The results of the auctions will be announced on October 15, 2001 and payment
by successful bidders will be during banking hours on October 16, 2001. The stocks will
qualify for the ready forward facility.
  Bids for underwriting of the Government Securities under auction can be
submitted by the ‘Primary Dealers in the Government Securities Market’ up to 12.30 p.m.
on October 13, 2001 to the CGM-in-Charge, Internal Debt Management Cell, Reserve
Bank of India, Central Office, Fort, Mumbai 400 001.
