The “Banking Statistics: Quarterly Handout – September 2000” providing data
on Aggregate Deposits and Gross Bank Credit of Scheduled Commercial Banks as on Last
Friday of September 2000, with various classifications, has been published by Reserve Bank
of India. The primary data have been collected from the Scheduled Commercial Banks
through the BSR-7 return. Data have been presented according to top hundred 
population groups, districts, states and bank groups.

Among the States/Union Territories, the annual ( point to point) growth rate  of

Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and West Bengal together accounted for a total share of 70.1 per
cent of Gross Bank Credit. Maharashtra alone    contributed 18.4 per cent of total deposits
and 26.8 per cent of total credit.
The  All-India  Credit-Deposit (C-D) ratio as on  last Friday of  September 2000
works out to 57.1 per cent. This ratio was  relatively high for Foreign Banks (79.2 per cent)
and  State Bank of India & its Associates (70.1 per cent). The C-D ratios were much lower
for Other Scheduled Commercial Banks (56.1 per cent) ,  Nationalised Banks at 50.2 per cent
