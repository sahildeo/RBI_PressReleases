March 29, 2001
The Reserve Bank of India has advised all primary cooperative banks that they
should close their annual accounts for the year ending March 31, 2001 on April 1, 2001.
 As such, primary urban cooperative banks will function normally on April 3,
2001 in those states where April 2, 2001 has been declared a holiday under the
Negotiable Instrument Act ,1881 on account of Ram Navami. In other states, the normal
working day for these banks will be April 2, 2001.
In other words, all scheduled commercial banks will, on April 1, 2001, close their
annual accounts for the year ending March 31, 2001 and will be open for public dealings
on April 2, 2001 if it is not a holiday or on April 3, 2001 if April 2 is a holiday for Ram
.
