Governor of Maharashtra visits RBI Archives, Pune 
 
His Excellency, Shri K. Sankaranarayanan,  the Governor  of Maharashtra visited 
the Reserve Bank of Indiaís Archives Museum, Pune, on August 14, 2010.  
Welcoming His Excellency, Shri Deepak Mohanty, Executive Director, Reserve 
Bank of India said that the archives not only nurtured the sentiments for culture 
and history of an organisation but also acted as a bridge between past, present 
and future. It was more so for the Reserve Bank as the financial and the 
economic history of the country was intertwined with the history of the Reserve 
Bank. He also said that in order to create public awareness, the Reserve Bank 
had recently set up the Archives Museum. The Museum was becoming an 
important attraction of Pune. 
His Excellency, Shri K. Sankaranarayanan remarked, ìI am so happy and 
delighted to be here in this institution. It (Museum) says that this is the history of 
our great country, very much useful to the students of the colleges in India and 
abroadî. He emphasised the need for making the present generation aware of 
the heritage of the country and therefore, urged for a wide publicity to be given to 
the RBI Archives Museum. 
Ms. Kamala Rajan, Principal of the College of Agricultural Banking where 
Archives Museum is located, proposed a vote of thanks. 
Senior officers of Government of Maharashtra and the Reserve Bank and the 
College of Agricultural Banking were present at the event.
