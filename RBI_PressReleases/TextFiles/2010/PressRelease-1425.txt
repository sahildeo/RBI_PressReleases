March 30, 2010 
 
RBI cautions public : Not to deposit money in unincorporated bodies 
 
It has come to the notice of the Reserve Bank of India that some 
individuals/firms/unincorporated association of individuals (unincorporated bodies) 
have been collecting deposits from the public by making tall promises of high 
returns. Some of them are stated to have vanished without repaying deposits. 
Under Section 45-S(1) of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, unincorporated 
bodies that are carrying on the business of a financial institution or whose principal 
business is that of receiving deposits are prohibited from accepting deposits from 
the public. Members of public are hereby cautioned not to deposit money with such 
unincorporated bodies. Persons depositing money with such unincorporated 
bodies would be doing so at their own risk.
