February 18, 2010 
 
RBI Board meets today at Patna  
 
The Central Board of the Reserve Bank of India met today in Patna after a 
span of six years. The meeting reviewed key economic, monetary and financial 
developments. 
 
Dr D. Subbarao, Governor, Reserve Bank of India chaired the Central Board 
meeting. Shri Y.H. Malegam, Prof. Suresh Tendulkar, Prof. U. R. Rao, Shri Lakshmi 
Chand, Shri H.P. Ranina, Smt. Shashi Rajagopalan, Shri Suresh Neotia,                  
Dr. A. Vaidyanathan, Prof M. M. Sharma and Shri Sanjay Labroo were present in the 
meeting of the Central Board. Deputy Governors of the Reserve Bank,                 
Smt. Shyamala Gopinath, Smt. Usha Thorat, Dr. K.C. Chakrabarty and                  
Dr. Subir Gokarn were also present. 
 
The Central Board of Directors of the Reserve Bank meets at least once every 
quarter. The Board has scheduled meetings in Mumbai, Chennai and Kolkata each 
year. The post-budget meeting of the Board, traditionally held in New Delhi, is 
addressed by the Union Finance Minister. The other meetings of the Board are held 
in the remaining state capitals by rotation. The main function of the Central Board of 
the Reserve Bank is to provide overall direction to its affairs. 
 
After the meeting of the Central Board, the Reserve Bank Governor met with 
senior officials of the State Government and controlling heads of banks represented 
in Bihar. The meeting focused on banking related issues, such as, increasing credit-
deposit ratio, increasing banking presence in under-banked areas through branch 
expansion as well as Banking Correspondent/Banking facilitator (BF/BC) model, 
problem of non-lending/low lending by private banks in Bihar and the issue of 
transparency in disposal of loan applications. 
  
Earlier, the Reserve Bank Governor called on Shri Nitish Kumar, the Honíble 
Chief Minister of Bihar on February17, 2010 and discussed issues of mutual interest, 
like inflation conditions, credit-deposit ratio and problems in credit dispensation and 
branch expansion. Aspects related to the legislative hurdles in mortgaging 
agricultural land for commercial purpose, development of the self help group (SHG) 
movement and introduction of electronic benefit transfer in Bihar were also 
discussed.
