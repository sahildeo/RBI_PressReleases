GoI announces the sale (re-issue) of
two dated securities through price based auctions
July 8, 2003
The Government of India has announced the sale (re-issue) of “6.35 percent
Government Stock 2020” for a notified amount of Rs. 6,000 crore and of “7.95 percent
Government Stock 2032” for a notified amount of Rs. 3,000 crore. Both the securities have
been offered for sale through price-based auction using multiple price auction method. The
Reserve Bank of India (RBI) will conduct the auctions at Mumbai on Tuesday, July 15, 2003.
 Up to 5 per cent of the notified amount of the sale of both the stocks will be allotted to
eligible individuals and institutions as per the Scheme for Non-Competitive Bidding Facility
in the Auction of Government Securities.
  Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve Bank of
India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort, Mumbai 400 001 should be submitted to
that Office on Tuesday, July 15, 2003. The competitive bids by the members of the
Negotiated Dealing System (NDS) should be submitted only in electronic format using NDS,
while all the non-competitive bids should be submitted in the prescribed physical form. All
bids should be submitted by 12.30 p.m. On the basis of bids received, the Reserve Bank will
determine the minimum price up to which offers will be accepted.
 The results of the auctions will be announced on July 15, 2003 and payment by
successful bidders will be during banking hours on July 16, 2003 (Wednesday). Both the
stocks will qualify for the ready forward facility.
 Bids for underwriting of the Government Securities under auction can be submitted
by the ‘Primary Dealers’ up to 12.30 p.m. on July 14, 2003 (Monday) to the Chief General
Manager, Internal Debt Management Department, Reserve Bank of India, Central Office,
Fort, Mumbai 400 001.
It may be recalled that the Government had, through its Press Release dated June 26,
2003 had announced that the aggregated notified amount for the auctions scheduled during
July 14-21, 2003 would be Rs. 9,000 crore instead of Rs. 5,000 crore announced in the
calendar.
