GoI to sell three dated securities through price based auctions;
Increases notified amount by Rs. 4000 crore for next auction
June 26, 2003
The Government of India has announced the sale (re-issue) of “7.37 per cent Government
Stock 2014” for a notified amount of Rs. 5,000 crore (nominal), “6.05 per cent Government Stock
2019” for a notified amount of Rs. 4,000 crore (nominal) and “6.13 per cent Government Stock
2028” for a notified amount of Rs. 3,000 crore (nominal). The Government Stocks will be sold
through price-based auctions using multiple price method.  The auctions will be conducted by the
Reserve Bank of India, Mumbai Office, Fort, Mumbai on Tuesday, July 1, 2003.
Up to 5 per cent of the notified amount of the sale of all the three stocks will be allotted
to eligible individuals and institutions as per the Scheme for Non-Competitive Bidding Facility
in the Auction of Government Securities.
Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve Bank of
India, Mumbai Office (Public Debt Office), Fort, Mumbai 400 001 should be submitted to that
Office on Tuesday, July 1, 2003. Competitive bids by the members of the Negotiated Dealing
System (NDS) should be submitted only in electronic format using NDS, while all the non-
competitive bids should be submitted in the prescribed physical form. All bids should be
submitted by 12.30 p.m. On the basis of bids received, the Reserve Bank will determine the
minimum price up to which offers will be accepted.
The results of the auctions will be announced on July 1, 2003 and payment by successful
bidders will be during banking hours on July 2, 2003 (Wednesday). All the three stocks will
qualify for the ready forward facility.
Bids for underwriting of the Government Securities under auction can be submitted by
the Primary Dealers up to 12.30 p.m. on Monday, June 30, 2003 to the Chief General Manager,
Internal Debt Management Department, Reserve Bank of India, Central Office, Fort, Mumbai
400 001.
It may be recalled that as per the calendar for issuance of Government securities the
notified amounts and the tenure of the securities for the auction scheduled during July 1-7, 2003
were Rs. 5,000 crore for a security with 10 year to 14 years tenure and Rs. 4,000 crore for a
security with 15 year to 19 year tenure. Taking into account all relevant factors, the Government
of India has, however, decided to sale (re-issue) a 25-year security for a notified amount of Rs.
3,000 crore in addition to the securities notified in the calendar.
The Government of India has also decided to increase the notified amount for the auctions
scheduled during July 14-21, 2003 by Rs. 4,000 crore to Rs. 9,000 crore as against Rs. 5,000
crore announced earlier. The details in this regard would be announced later.
