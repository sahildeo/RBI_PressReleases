On Tap Sale of State Government Loans
on February 25, 2003
February 20, 2003
The first tranche of the market borrowings for Rs.7,325 crore will be on February 25,
2003. The ten-year securities will carry a coupon rate of 6.95 per cent. In case of
oversubscription, the States will have the option to retain an additional amount up to 25 per cent
of the target amount.
The Reserve Bank of India reserves the right to close the sale during the business hours
on or after February 25, 2003 having regard to the requirements of the State Governments and
the subscriptions received.
The sale for all the 27 States will be held at the respective Regional Offices of the
Reserve Bank of India and at Mumbai. Applications in the prescribed form can be obtained from
the Public Debt Office at the Reserve Bank of India and will be received at the concerned
Regional Offices during office hours while the sale is open.
Market participants were informed on February 19, 2003 that the State Governments
would be accessing the market for additional borrowings for an amount of Rs.14,151 crore in
two tranches in the current financial year. The amount includes Rs.10,000 crore for the debt
swap scheme mutually agreed between the Central Government and the State governments
towards repayment of high cost debt owed by the States to the Centre.
