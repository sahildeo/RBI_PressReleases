April 21, 1999
The Reserve Bank of India has granted general permission to foreign airline
companies under the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 to carry on their
commercial activities through their local agents on certain conditions.  The foreign airline
companies will now be able to carry on their commercial activities, such as,
transportation of goods and passengers, and collection of freight charges and fares for
online and offline operations through their local agents without obtaining prior approval
To operate under general permission, the foreign airline should have obtained a
permission from the Director General of Civil Aviation in respect of online operations;
and there should be a bilateral air service agreement between the Government of India
and the Government of the country of incorporation of the foreign airline company in
