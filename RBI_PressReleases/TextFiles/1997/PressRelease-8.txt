Reverse repos with banks allowed for 19 non-bank entities 
The Government of India has permitted 19 non-bank entities to enter into reverse repo 
transactions in treasury bills of all maturities issued by the Government of India and in 
such dated securities of the Government of India as are approved by the Reserve Bank 
of India, in consultation with the Central Government. The transactions will, however, 
have to be undertaken only at Mumbai and put through the Subsidiary General Ledger 
Account with the Reserve Bank of India. The non-bank entities are:  
1. Canbank Mutual Fund  
2. Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India Ltd.  
3. Export Import Bank of India  
4. General Insurance Corporation of India Ltd.  
5. GIC Mutual Fund  
6. Indian Bank Mutual Fund  
7. Industrial Credit & Investment Corporation of India Ltd.  
8. Industrial Development Bank of India  
9. IDBI Mutual Fund  
10. ITC Thread Needle Mutual Fund  
11. Life Insurance Corporation of India  
12. National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development  
13. National Housing Bank  
14. New India Assurance Company Ltd.  
15. Oriental Insurance Company Ltd.  
16. Reliance Capital Mutual Fund  
17. SBI Mutual Fund  
18. Small Industries Development Bank of India  
19. Unit Trust of India.
