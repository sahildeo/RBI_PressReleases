October 15, 2012  
RBI cautions Public Not to respond to Phishing Mail sent in its Name 
It has come to the notice of the Reserve Bank of India that an email has been 
sent in its name from mail id: Reserve Bank Of India <no-reply@rbi.com> and signed 
by RBI, Security Team offering a 'new online security protection' called "Netsecured” 
to “reduce fraud and theft in various banking system…(and)… to enable all 
customer's online banking in all Indian Banks to get protected and Secured.” 
The Reserve Bank cautions members of public that it has not developed any 
such software; nor has it sent any such mail asking online banking customers to 
update their account details to secure their online accounts. In fact, the Reserve 
Bank does not have any mail id with extension @rbi.com  
Members of public receiving such mails should not open the attachment 
and/or try to download the attachment on their computers. This is a phishing mail 
and accessing the mail in any manner could result in identity theft.
