Sources of Variation in Foreign Exchange Reserves in India during 2011-12 
Earlier today, the Reserve Bank of India released the Balance of Payments 
(BoP) data for Q4 of 2011-12 (January-March 2012) on its website (www.rbi.org.in). 
On the basis of these data, the sources of variation in foreign exchange reserves 
during 2011-12 (April-March) have been compiled. 
Sources of Variation in Foreign Exchange Reserves: 2011-12 (April-March) 
During 2011-12, there was a decline in the foreign exchange reserves. The 
sources of variation in the foreign exchange reserves are set out in Table 1.

On a balance of payments basis (i.e., excluding valuation effects), the foreign 
exchange reserves declined by US$ 12.8 billion during 2011-12 as against an 
increase of US$ 13.1 billion during the previous year. The foreign exchange reserves 
(including the valuation effects) declined by US$ 10.4 billion during 2011-12 as 
against an increase of US$ 25.8 billion during the previous year (Table 2).
