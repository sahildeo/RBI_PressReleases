July 18, 2008 
RBI releases 'Quarterly Statistics on Deposits and Credit of  
Scheduled Commercial Banks: March 2008' 
The Reserve Bank of India today released its 'Quarterly Statistics on Deposits and Credit 
of Scheduled Commercial Banks-March 2008'. The publication contains data relating to deposits 
and credit of scheduled commercial banks including regional rural banks (RRBs) as on March 31, 
2008. 
'Quarterly Statistics on Deposits and Credit of Scheduled Commercial Banks' provides 
data on aggregate deposits and gross bank credit of scheduled commercial banks as on March 31, 
2008. The data are based on Basic Statistical Return (BSR)-7, received from the branches of all 
scheduled commercial banks (including RRBs).  
 
Highlights: 
Number of banked centres and deposits and credit of top 100/200 centres 
The number of banked centres served by scheduled commercial banks as on March 31, 
2008 stood at 34,426. Of these centres, 28,529 were single office centres and 54 centres had 100 
or more bank offices.  
The top hundred centres, out of 34,426 banked centres, arranged according to the size of 
deposits accounted for 69.7 per cent of the total deposits and the top hundred centres arranged 
according to the size of bank credit accounted for 77.8 per cent of total bank credit. In March 2007, 
the corresponding share of top hundred centres in aggregate deposits and gross bank credit were 
68.9 per cent and 77.4 per cent, respectively. Aggregate deposits of top hundred centres 
increased by 25.7 per cent in March 2008 over March 2007 compared to a growth of 27.7 per cent 
recorded a year ago. Growth rate of gross bank credit of top hundred centres at 23.4 per cent in 
March 2008 was much lower compared to 30.0 per cent growth recorded in March 2007.  
The Top 200 centres in terms of aggregate deposits covered 30.7 per cent of reporting 
offices and 74.6 per cent of aggregate deposits. The Top 200 centres in terms of gross bank 
credit, accounted for 81.4 per cent of gross bank credit and 30.3 percent of reporting offices. 
 
Bank-group wise shares in deposits and credit 
Nationalised Banks, as a group, accounted for 48.5 per cent of the aggregate deposits, 
while State Bank of India and its associates accounted for 22.6 per cent. The share of other 
scheduled commercial banks, foreign banks and RRBs in aggregate deposits were 20.3 per cent, 
5.6 per cent and 3.0 per cent, respectively. As regards gross bank credit, nationalised banks held 
the maximum share of 48.3 per cent in the total bank credit followed by State Bank of India and its 
associates at 22.8 per cent and other scheduled commercial banks at 19.7 per cent. Foreign 
banks and RRBs had relatively lower share in the total bank credit at 6.8 per cent and 2.4 per cent, 
respectively.  Credit-Deposit ratio: State/Union territory-wise and Bank-group wise 
At the All–India level, the credit-deposit (C-D) ratio of all scheduled commercial banks as 
on March 31, 2008 stood at 74.2 per cent. Among the States/Union Territories, the highest C-D 
ratio was observed in Tamil Nadu (113.4 per cent) followed by Maharashtra (94.7 per cent). At the 
bank group level, the C-D ratio was above the All-India ratio in respect of foreign banks (89.9 per 
cent) and State Bank of India and its associates (74.9 per cent), and was lower in case of 
nationalised banks (73.9 per cent), other scheduled commercial banks (71.7) and Regional Rural 
Banks (60.2 per cent). As regards population group-wise C-D ratio of all scheduled commercial 
banks, metropolitan centres had the highest C-D ratio ( 86.9 per cent), followed distantly by rural 
centres 60.4 per cent) and urban centres (58.3 per cent). The semi-urban centres recorded the 
lowest C-D ratio at 53.7 per cent. 
Distribution of bank offices by size of their deposits and credit 
The distribution of scheduled commercial bank offices by size of deposits, indicates that 
the offices with deposits of Rs.10 crore or more, accounted for 59.2 per cent of the bank offices, 
95.3 per cent in terms of aggregate deposits and 92.1 per cent in total bank credit. The offices, 
which extended credit of Rs.10 crore or more, accounted for 36.2 per cent in terms of total number 
of offices. These offices together accounted for 91.7 per cent of total bank credit whereas their 
share in aggregate deposits was 75.9 per cent.
