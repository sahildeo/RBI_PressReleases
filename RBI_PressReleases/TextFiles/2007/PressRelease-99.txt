December 17, 2008 
 
RBI's Special Fixed Rate Term Repo Auction for Rs. 57,130 Crore Today 
 
The Reserve Bank of India shall conduct a special fixed rate term repo at 6.5 per cent 
per annum against eligible securities for Rs.57,130 crore on December 17, 2008, due for 
reversal on December 31, 2008, with a view to enabling banks to meet the liquidity 
requirements of Mutual Funds (MFs), Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) and Housing 
Finance Companies (HFCs) either on incremental or on rollover basis. Of the outstanding 
amount of Rs.4,820 crore as on December 16, 2008, Rs.1,950 crore is due for redemption 
today. 
 
It may be recalled that the Reserve Bank of India had announced earlier that it would 
conduct the special fixed rate term repo under Liquidity Adjustment Facility daily till June 30, 
2009 up to a cumulative amount of Rs.60,000 crore on outstanding basis and that additional 
liquidity support to the extent of up to 1.5 per cent of their NDTL, can be availed by banks 
exclusively for the purpose of meeting requirements of MFs, NBFCs and HFCs.
