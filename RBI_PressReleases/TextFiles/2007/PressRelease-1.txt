December 31, 2008 
 
Seven State Governments announce Auction of State Development Loans 2019 on 
January 6, 2009 for Rs. 5,177.100 crore 
 
The following State Governments have announced the sale of their 10-year State 
Development Loans (SDLs) for an aggregate amount of Rs. 5,177.100 crore through a yield 
based auction using multiple price auction method.  
 
Sr. No. State Notified Amount 
(Rs. crore) 
1 Andhra Pradesh 1,000.000 
2 Gujarat    1,500.000 
3 Himachal Pradesh 200.000 
4 Jharkhand 248.100 
5 Kerala 812.000 
6 Madhya Pradesh 750.000 
7 Punjab 667.000 
 Total   5,177.100 
 
Note: Governments of Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh will have the option to 
retain additional subscription upto a maximum of Rs.375.00 crore and 
Rs.150.000 crore respectively over and above their respective notified amount. 
 
The auction will be conducted by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) at Mumbai on January 
6, 2009 (Tuesday). Bids in the prescribed form obtainable from the Regional Director, Reserve 
Bank of India, Public Debt Office, Main Building, Fort, Mumbai- 400 001 should be submitted to 
that Office on Tuesday, January 6, 2009. The competitive bids by the NDS members should be 
submitted only in electronic format using Primary Market Operation (PMO) module of NDS. All 
bids should be submitted by 12.30 P.M. on January 6, 2009.  On the basis of bids received, RBI 
will determine the maximum yield at which offers will be accepted. 
 
The results of the auction will be announced on January 6, 2009 and payment by 
successful bidders will be made during banking hours on January 7, 2009 (Wednesday) at 
Mumbai and at respective Regional Offices of Reserve Bank of India.  
 
The investment in Government Stocks will be reckoned as an eligible investment in 
Government Securities by banks for the purpose of Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) under 
Section 24 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949. The stocks will qualify for the ready forward 
facility.
