May 19, 2004
States to tap market for Rs.8000 crore on May 26
Nineteen State Governments have offered to sell on tap 5.70 per cent
State Development Loan, 2014 having maturity of ten years on May 26, 2004.
For an aggregate target amount of Rs. 8000 crore, the States will have  an option
to retain up to 20 per cent of excess subscription, on account of debt swap
scheme in the current financial year.
The States that will tap the market are viz., Andhra Pradesh, Assam,
Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir,
Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Orissa, Punjab,
Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh and  West Bengal. In addition, states of
Kerala and Tamil Nadu will be raising an aggregate amount of Rs. 380 crore as a
part of their normal market borrowings.
The sale will be held at the respective regional offices of the Reserve Bank
of India and at Mumbai (for all the 19 States).  The Reserve Bank of India
reserves the right to close the sale during the banking hours on or after May 26,
2004 having regard to the requirements of the State Governments and the
subscriptions received. The date of payment will be May 28, 2004.
Applications in the prescribed form obtainable from the Public Debt Office
at the Reserve Bank of India will be received at the Reserve Bank offices during
business hours while the sale is open.
