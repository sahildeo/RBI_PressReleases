November 3, 2004
Tap sale of 7.36 per cent State Development Loans, 2014 closed
The sale `on tap' of 7.36 per cent State Development Loan, 2014 in respect of
eighteen States, viz., Andhra Pradesh, Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Bihar, Haryana,
Jammu & Kashmir, Jharkhand, Maharashtra, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland,
Orissa, Rajasthan, Sikkim, Tripura, Uttar Pradesh and Uttaranchal which was kept open
on November 3, 2004, has been closed today.
It may be recalled that the Governments of 25 States had offered to sell 7.36 per
cent State Development Loans, 2014 aggregating about Rs.6200 crore on November 2,
2004, by way of tap sale under the normal market borrowing programme.
The tap sale in respect of seven States, viz., Goa, Gujarat, Himachal Pradesh,
Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and West Bengal had closed on November 2,
2004 on receipt of respective target amounts.
As notified earlier, payment will be required to be made by the applicants on
November 4, 2004 in respect of allotments made against subscription applications
received both on November 2 and November 3, 2004.
Of the total subscriptions of Rs.5621 crore received, Rs.5084 crore has been
retained.
