July 22, 2004
On Tap Sale of States Government Loans
for Rs.8600 crore, on July 28
Twenty State Governments have offered to sell on tap 6.35% State
Development Loan 2013 (II Series) by way of tap sale on July 28, 2004 for an
aggregate amount of about Rs.8600 crore.
Of this, nineteen States will tap the market for an aggregate amount of
about Rs.8000 crore as the second tranche of additional market borrowing on
account of Debt Swap Scheme in the current financial year and three states
will raise an aggregate amount of Rs.638.45 crore as part of their normal
market borrowings.
The States that will tap the market are, viz., Andhra Pradesh, Assam,
Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir,
Jharkhand, Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Orissa,
Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal. States going
in for normal market borrowings are Madhya Pradesh, Nagaland and Punjab.
The States will have an option to retain up to 20 per cent of excess
subscription, on account of debt swap scheme in the current financial year.
The sale will be held at the respective regional offices of the Reserve
Bank of India and at Mumbai (for all the 20 States).  The Reserve Bank of
India reserves the right to close the sale during the banking hours on or after
July 28, 2004 having regard to the requirements of the State Governments
and the subscriptions received. The date of payment will be July 30, 2004.
Applications in the prescribed form obtainable from the Public Debt
Office at the Reserve Bank of India will be received at the Reserve Bank
offices during business hours while the sale is open.
