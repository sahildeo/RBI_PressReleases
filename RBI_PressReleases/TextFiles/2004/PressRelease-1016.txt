RBI advises Banks to accept Coins by Weight
Members of public can now tender current and uncurrent coins at the counters of public
and private sector banks as also at the Reserve Bank of India's counters.  Banks and the
Reserve Bank will accept these coins by weight.
The Reserve Bank of India has advised all the branches of public/private sector banks in
Mumbai city to accept, by weight, aluminium coins of 5 paise, 10 paise, 20 paise, aluminium
bronze coins of 10 paise, stainless coins of 10 paise, cupronickel coins of 25 paise, 50 paise and
rupee one denomination. The Reserve Bank has requested the members of public having stocks
of such coins to tender them, denomination-wise and metal-wise, over the counters in lots of 100
coins in polythene sachets. Similar arrangements also exist for acceptance of current stainless
steel coins of 25 paise, 50 paise and one rupee and cupronickel coins of two rupees and five
rupees.
