RBI releases Report on the Progress and Agenda
for IMF’s Standards and Codes
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) today, placed on its website, the "Review of the
Recommendations of the Advisory Groups Constituted by the Standing Committee on
International Financial Standards And Codes: Report on the Progress and Agenda
Ahead ". The Reserve Bank has invited views on the Report to helpmpd@rbi.org.in or to
Adviser-in-Charge, Monetary Policy Department (MPD) on fax no. 022-22651685 or 022-
22693204.
The Report reviews the progress, provides the current status on the implementation and
captures new developments in the field of international financial standards and codes.
The objective of the review is to stimulate further discussions and improve awareness
about the international standards and codes among various stakeholders.
It may be recalled that in May 2002, the Reports of the eleven Advisory/Technical
Groups were published and also placed on the RBI website, along with the Report of the
Standing Committee that included a synthesis report prepared by an outside expert. The
Advisory/Technical Groups constituted by the Standing Committee on International
Financial Standards and Codes, consisted of members who were almost wholly experts
from outside the Reserve Bank as well as outside the Government, The Mid-Term
Review of Annual Policy Statement for 2004-05 had indicated that the Report of the
progress made on the implementation of the recommendations of these eleven Groups
would be placed in the public domain for wider dissemination.
