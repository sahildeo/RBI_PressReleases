Senior Citizens Savings Scheme, 2004
operationalised through Public Sector Banks
The Government of India had introduced Senior Citizens Savings Scheme,
2004 (SCSS) with effect from August 2, 2004 through designated Post Offices. The
Government has now decided to operate the scheme also through designated
branches of 23 public sector banks (presently operating Public Provident Fund
Scheme, 1968) with effect from November 1, 2004. The Reserve Bank of India has
suitably advised the agency banks, viz., State Bank of India, Associate banks, 14
banks nationalised in 1969 and Corporation Bank in this regard. Members of public
may contact the officials of designated branches of public sector banks for
investment in the scheme/guidance.  For convenience of the senior
citizens/investors, the details/general rules, etc., of the Senior Citizens Savings
Scheme, 2004 have been placed on the RBI web site (http://www.goisb.rbi.org.in).
