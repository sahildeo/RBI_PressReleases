February 10, 2004 
 
Tap Sale of State Government Loans  
 
 Eighteen State Governments have offered to sell 5.85 per cent State 
Development Loan, 2015 having maturity of eleven years aggregating about  Rs.4300 
crore on February 19, 2004 by way of tap sale. Of this, nine States, viz., Chattisgarh, 
Karnataka, Kerala, Madhya Pradesh, Orissa, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu and West 
Bengal will be raising Rs.3500 crore as additional market borrowings and other nine 
States, viz., Arunachal Pradesh, Bihar, Maharashtra, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, 
Nagaland, Tripura and Uttar Pradesh will be raising about Rs.800 crore under the Debt 
Swap Scheme. The sale will be held at the respective regional offices of the Reserve 
Bank of India and at Mumbai (for all the 18 States) on February 19, 2004. The Reserve 
Bank of India reserves the right to close the sale during or after close of banking hours 
on or after February 19, 2004 having regard to the subscriptions received. The payment 
by the successful bidders will be made on February 21, 2004. 
 
 Applications in the prescribed form obtainable from the Public Debt Office at the 
Reserve Bank of India will be received at the aforesaid offices during banking hours 
while the sale is open.
